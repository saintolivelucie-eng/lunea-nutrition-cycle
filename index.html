Phase 1 · Days 1–5

Menstrual — Rest & Replenish

Your body is working incredibly hard right now, support it with some warm and iron-rich meals, and allow for some rest.

1–5

Cycle Days

What's happening in your body

Estrogen and progesterone have both dropped to their lowest point. Your uterine lining is shedding. This is real work — your body is doing a full reset.

What your body needs most

Iron (you're losing it while bleeding), magnesium (to ease cramps), omega-3s (to calm inflammation). Warm & slow meals.
No pressure to perform.

How you may feel

Like someone pulled your plug. That 3pm walk you managed last week? Today it might be your whole workout — and that's genuinely fine.

🌑

Progesterone

LH / FSH

Both hormones at their lowest. This is why you need rest — not because you're weak, because your body is literally in restoration mode and needs you to listen to it.

✨ Key Nutrients

🩸 Iron🐟 Omega-3🥜 Magnesium🌿 Vitamin C💜 B Vitamins🫙 Zinc
💡 Always pair iron foods with vitamin C — it triples absorption. Have that spinach with lemon.
💡 Aim for oily fish 2–3× per week during menstrual & luteal phases.

🛡️ Condition guidance

🔴

Autoimmune conditions

Inflammatory markers can spike. Prioritise turmeric, ginger, omega-3s. Remove gluten if you have Hashimoto's.

🌸

PCOS

Keep blood sugar stable with slow carbs. Avoid refined sugar — it worsens androgen spikes.

🌺

Endometriosis

Anti-inflammatory foods are critical this week. Omega-3s, cruciferous veg, no alcohol.

🦋

Thyroid conditions

Selenium-rich foods (Brazil nuts) support thyroid function. Avoid raw cruciferous if Hashimoto's.

Phase 3 · Days 14–16

Ovulatory — Peak Vitality

3 days where everuthing clicks. Your body, your mood and your drive to achieve your goals. Ride this wave and have some light, raw foods to keep the flow going.

14–16

Cycle Days

What's happening in your body

Estrogen peaks, LH surges, an egg is released. Your body temperature rises slightly. You're literally at your physiological best right now.

What your body needs most

Antioxidants to protect the egg, selenium for hormone sensitivity, fiber to clear estrogen. Light & raw foods help to extend this phase.

How you may feel

Remember running that 5k two weeks ago and feeling like you were dying? You could probably do it twice right now. That's not imagination, that's estrogen.

🔴

🦋

Thyroid conditions

Good week for Brazil nuts (selenium). Maintain consistent iodine intake. Avoid excessive raw goitrogenic foods.

Phase 4 · Days 17–28

Luteal — Ground & Restore

The phase that is the most underlooked, but oh so important to prep your body for the next menstrual phase. Magnesium and slow carbs are your allies to prevent PMS and cravings.

17–28

Cycle Days

What's happening in your body

Progesterone rises (then falls at the end). Digestion slows. Appetite increases. Your body is preparing for either pregnancy or your next period — it's doing a lot.

What your body needs most

Magnesium (your biggest ally against PMS), B6, complex carbs that stabilise serotonin. More calories are normal and appropriate in this phase. Feed yourself.

How you may feel

That run that felt easy last week? Your heart rate is actually higher now for the same effort. Your body is genuinely working harder. You're not imagining it.

🍂

📊 Hormone Levels

Estrogen
Falling
Progesterone
Peak

When progesterone drops in late luteal, serotonin drops with it. That's PMS. Magnesium and B6 are proven to help.

✨ Key Nutrients

🥜 Magnesium🍠 Complex carbs🌿 Vitamin B6🦴 Calcium🌸 Tryptophan

Tryptophan converts to serotonin. Turkey, chickpeas, banana — nature's mood stabilisers.

🛡️ Condition guidance

🔴

Autoimmune conditions

Late luteal can increase inflammatory activity. Selenium (Brazil nuts), magnesium, turmeric. Rest is non-negotiable.

🌸

PCOS

This is the most important phase for blood sugar stability. Chromium-rich foods (broccoli, eggs) help with insulin sensitivity.

🌺

Endometriosis

Avoid dairy, alcohol, and processed meats completely. Omega-3s and magnesium for pain management.

🦋

Eat your way through it

Eat to prevent & relieve your symptoms

For each symptom, here's what to eat in the days before it hits. And what to reach for when it's already there. Food-first, always.

📅

Days 17–28 · Luteal phase

Prevent it — eat this before your period

Cramps are caused by prostaglandins — inflammatory compounds released when your period starts. Build up the right nutrients in week 4 and you'll feel the difference next month.
🐟

Salmon & sardines 3×/week

EPA/DHA omega-3 directly blocks prostaglandin synthesis

🥜

Almonds & pumpkin seeds daily

Magnesium pre-loads the uterine muscle — the earlier you start, the better

🍫

Dark chocolate (70%+)

One of the richest dietary magnesium sources — and the craving your body is getting right

✕

Avoid: alcohol, refined sugar, trans fats

All three increase prostaglandin production

🩸

Days 1–5 · Menstrual phase

Relieve it — eat this when it hits

When cramps are already there: heat + magnesium + ginger. That combination is the most effective food-first approach. Studies show ginger is comparable to ibuprofen for period pain.
🫚

Fresh ginger tea (3 cups/day)

Clinical studies show it's comparable to ibuprofen — steep 1cm fresh ginger for 10 min

🍵

Chamomile tea

Glycine directly relaxes uterine muscle spasms

🍲

Warm soups & bone broth

The warmth alone relaxes smooth muscle — glycine adds anti-spasmodic effect

✕

Avoid: coffee, cold foods & dairy

Coffee constricts blood vessels. Cold food slows digestion and worsens cramping

📅

Days 17–24 · Early luteal

Prevent it — eat this before PMS builds

PMS is driven by falling estrogen and progesterone combined with serotonin instability. The right nutrients in early luteal prevent most of it from developing.
🍠

Sweet potato

Richest food source of B6 — clinically proven to reduce PMS severity by up to 69%

🌾

Oats in the morning

Stable blood sugar = stable serotonin. Skipping breakfast in week 4 is a direct PMS trigger

🥜

Magnesium-rich snacks daily

Low magnesium is the most common nutritional driver of PMS — most women are deficient

✕

Avoid: caffeine, alcohol, refined sugar

All three deplete magnesium and destabilise blood sugar simultaneously

🩸

Days 25–28 · Late luteal

Relieve it — eat this when PMS peaks

When PMS is already in full swing: serotonin-supporting, magnesium-dense foods. Your body genuinely needs more calories in this phase — feeding yourself well is not indulging, it's medicine.
🍫

Dark chocolate + almonds

Magnesium + serotonin precursors — eat this, not a processed snack

🍌

Banana with nut butter

B6 + tryptophan + healthy fats — directly raises serotonin

🍵

Chamomile or saffron tea

Saffron has clinical evidence for PMS mood support at 30mg/day

✕

Avoid: salty snacks & processed dairy

Salt worsens bloating. Dairy increases prostaglandins at the worst possible time

📅

Ongoing prevention

Prevent it — eat this before bloating starts

Hormonal bloating is caused by progesterone slowing digestion combined with fluid retention. A healthy gut microbiome dramatically reduces how much you bloat all month.
🫙

Yogurt & Fermented foods at hands

Kimchi, sauerkraut, kefir — a diverse gut processes gas-producing foods far more efficiently

💧

Lean protein such as chicken, fish and legumes

Provide essential nutrients to help the gut works its best

2+ litres of water daily

Counterintuitively, more water reduces water retention — dehydration makes it worse

✕

Avoid: excess salt, carbonated drinks

Salt retains fluid. Fizzy drinks introduce gas directly

🩸

When it happens

Relieve it — eat this when you're bloated

When bloating is already there: speed up digestion, release trapped gas, reduce fluid retention. Skip raw veg today — choose cooked, easily digestible options.
🍵

Peppermint tea immediately

Menthol relaxes gut smooth muscle — fastest natural remedy for trapped gas

🫚

Fresh ginger with warm water

Speeds gastric emptying — reduces distension within 20–30 minutes

✕

Avoid: beans, cruciferous veg, dairy today

Even healthy foods can be high gas producers — switch to courgette, carrot, sweet potato

📅

Prevention

Prevent it — build your energy reserves first

Cycle fatigue is mostly caused by iron loss during menstruation and magnesium depletion in luteal. Keeping both topped up consistently is far more effective than dealing with the crash.
🥬

Spinach & lentils with citrus

Always pair iron with vitamin C — citrus triples absorption

🥚

Eggs for breakfast

Complete protein + B12 + D3 — the most energy-dense single food for cycle fatigue

✕

Avoid: coffee near iron foods & refined carbs

Coffee blocks iron absorption for up to 1 hour after eating

🩸

When exhaustion hits

Relieve it — eat this when you're drained

When fatigue is already there: steady energy, no spikes, no crashes. Avoid stimulants. Choose whole foods with protein, slow carbs and iron.
🍵

Matcha over coffee

4–6 hours of calm, focused energy without the iron-blocking & the afternoon crash

🍌

Banana with almond butter

Fast natural sugar + protein/fat — noticeable energy lift within 15 minutes

✕

Avoid: energy drinks, white bread, sugary snacks

They spike then crash — the cycle of fatigue gets worse, not better

📅

Days 17–28 · Luteal

Prevent it — eat this before breakouts appear

Hormonal acne is triggered by androgens rising in luteal phase. A low-glycaemic, zinc-rich, anti-inflammatory diet reduces breakouts significantly over 4–6 consistent weeks.
🌰

Brazil nuts & pumpkin seeds

Selenium + zinc — directly linked to sebum regulation and skin cell turnover

🥦

Cruciferous vegetables

DIM (natural compound formed from the digestion) supports liver clearance and acts as an antioxidant

✕

Avoid: dairy, high-sugar foods, seed oils

Dairy raises IGF-1. Sugar spikes insulin — the direct driver of hormonal acne

🩸

When breakouts are active

Calm it — eat this during a flare

When skin is already breaking out: reduce systemic inflammation and support the liver's ability to clear hormones. Nothing that spikes blood sugar.
🐟

Salmon & sardines

EPA reduces the specific compound that drives acne lesion formation

💧

3 litres of water daily

Flushes excess hormones via the kidneys — direct impact on skin load

✕

Avoid: alcohol, dairy, processed foods

Alcohol stresses the liver which can't then clear androgens

Days 17–26 · Build stability early

Prevent it — build serotonin reserves

Mood swings are caused by falling estrogen reducing serotonin, combined with blood sugar instability. A consistent diet that keeps blood sugar flat prevents most of the volatility.
🫙

Fermented foods daily

90% of serotonin is made in the gut — a healthy microbiome is your best mood stabiliser

🌾

Complex Carbs

Whole-grain pasta and brown rice to provide steady energy and prevent the sudden crashes

🍌

Banana daily in luteal phase

B6 + tryptophan — direct serotonin precursors. Takes 3–5 days of consistent intake to feel

✕

Avoid: alcohol, ultra-processed food, skipping meals

All three crash blood sugar and disrupt the gut-brain axis simultaneously

🩸

When you're in it

Ease it — eat this when mood dips hit

When mood has already dipped: fast-acting serotonin and dopamine support. These foods work within hours. Alcohol feels like it helps — it doesn't. It makes it worse by morning.
🍫

Dark chocolate

Phenylethylamine + anandamide — genuine immediate mood-lifting compounds

🐟

Salmon & sardines

Omega-3 fatty acids are known to reduce inflamation and depression symptoms

🫖

Saffron tea

Clinical trials show saffron comparable to low-dose antidepressants for PMS mood — 30mg, two cups daily

✕

Avoid: alcohol and caffeine when mood is low

Both worsen mood instability within hours

📅

Days 20–28 · Luteal prevention

Prevent it — eat this for better sleep all week

Progesterone rising and falling disrupts deep sleep in luteal. Magnesium and tryptophan eaten consistently during this phase dramatically improve sleep quality.
🥜

Almonds or Pistachios as evening snack

Provide both melatonin and magnesium, activating the parasympathetic nervous system — 1 handful before bed measurably improves sleep onset

🍵

Chamomile tea 30 min before bed

Apigenin binds GABA receptors — creates mild sedative effect

✕

Avoid: caffeine after 2pm, alcohol at night

Caffeine has a 6-hour half-life. Alcohol disrupts REM sleep even when it feels sedating

🩸

When sleep is broken

Improve it — eat this the day of poor sleep

After a bad night: prevent the blood sugar and cortisol crash. Eat slow, grounding foods. Try to avoid powering through with caffeine to go back to a normal sleeping pattern.
🥣

Warm oats with banana for breakfast

Slow carbs + tryptophan stabilise the cortisol spike that follows poor sleep

🍵

Matcha instead of coffee

L-theanine smooths caffeine without the cortisol spike that follows a bad night

✕

Avoid: more than one coffee, napping after 3pm

Multiple coffees worsen the next night. Late naps reset sleep pressure entirely
